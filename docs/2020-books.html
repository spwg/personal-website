2020 Reading List

Title Thoughts
The Autobiography of Charles Darwin: 1809-1882 Good summary and encapsulation of the life of Darwin. Doesn't focus at all on the HMS Beagle voyage, which was surprising. The footnotes are really interesting because they explain how Darwin's family edited his autobiography to reflect more acceptable views towards religion. The language and word choices they make are subtle.
The Splendid and the Vile: A Saga of Churchill, Family, and Defiance During the Blitz Focuses on people during the 1st year of Churchill's 1st Prime Ministership. It's interesting because the author quotes diaries — which everyone seemed to keep — that show people carrying on normal life as best they can, given the circumstances of nightly bombings. For example, a private secretary and Churchill's daughter played tennis after finding a German plane in the English countryside. The book's pacing is pleasant. It surprised me how the author dove right into dramatic episodes of the war among daily life. For example, important British battles against Rommel in North Africa at Tobruk basically occur during the weekend Churchill's daughter got engaged on a 2nd date and then got cold feet. Learned some new stories about how FDR sent men like William Harriman and Harry Hopkins to Britain in 1941.
So Good They Can't Ignore You A friend recommended this book. It basically says that passion is a bad guide in career development because it's not what most people who actually have successful careers used in their lives. Instead, you should build career capital — which is rare and valuable skills — over time in order to get great at what you do. Then you'll love what you do because you're great at it. You hone rare and valuable skills with deliberate practice, studying something formally, like in professional chess where there is a literature about the game. White collar workers can create their own deliberate practice too by emulating disciplines where there is formal study. It should feel hard, like you're really learning something.
Designing Data Intensive Applications About designing applications that can handle Web-scale traffic — hundreds of millions of queries per second, or an equivalently huge amount of data — while being reliable, scalable, and maintainable. Fault-tolerance, data models, query languages, storage & retrieval, encoding & schema evolution, replication, partitioning, transactions, distributed systems, consistency & consensus, batch processing, and stream processing.
