2018 Reading List

Title Thoughts
Innovator's Dilemma Very cited, you probably know it already
When Genius Failed Heard about these guys a lot, more detail here
Evolving Ourselves Juan Enriquez (author) is a VC and known for life science, which is fascinating if you want to live longer
Siddhartha Found through Naval Ravikant, it is an awesome story for the sake of storytelling and made me feel heavy, like I needed to digest the story after listening
Skin in the Game Classic Taleb
The Hard Thing About Hard Things Ben Horowitz is the Horowitz in Andreessen Horowitz
Things a Little Bird Told Me Written by a Twitter cofounder, the impression I got is he is a stereotypically hard charging founder
The Idea Factory About Bell Labs, the R&D dept. at AT&T, which invented the transistor, laser, information theory, and C
On the Shortness of Life Written by Seneca about 2,000 years ago but ageless
The Airbnb Story Good instance of technology versus politics ala Peter Thiel
Red Notice Super relevant story about Russia, corruption, government, private property, and Putin
How to Turn Down a Billion Dollars Cool to get another perspective on Snapchat and consumer internet innovation
Influence Pretty much everything you need to know about persuasion
Six Easy Pieces The 20% of work for 80% of knowledge (Pareto Principle) in fundamental physics (taught by Feynman)
Vagabonding Tim Ferris recommendation; full of practical and philosophical advice nominally about traveling
Perfectly Reasonable Deviations Another Feynman one, this time more about science in general but also life, philosophy, etc.
How Luck Happens I saw the author of this book at the Milken Scholars Summit 2018; this is basically about outcomes as probability distributions
Leonardo da Vinci Another awesome Isaacson biography, tinged by the fact I had recently been to Italy (including Florence) when I listened to it
The Three Body Problem Pretty crazy Chinese science fiction book that makes day-to-day life seem trivial
The Last Days of Night Electricity war; Edison, Tesla, Westinghouse; patent lawyers; X is value an invetor creates, Y is % of X captured by inventor, X and Y independent variables
Let My People Go Surfing Patagonia founder's autobio, part startup founding story, part pro-environmentalist anti-consumerist argument
Dealers of Lightning Story of Xerox PARC, which invented and/or developed laser printing, Ethernet, the PC, the GUI, object oriented programming, and VSLI
The House of Getty J. Paul Getty grew up in LA, founded a billion-dollar oil company whose sale caused a lawsuit for $10,500,000,000 (in 1985 dollars), ran his company for many years from another continent, and left $1,200,000,000 to a museum without telling anyone there
Getting to Yes This book is so unmemorable all I remember about it is that I think the author is some business negotiator bigshot
Play Bigger I visited a friend at Stanford for a weekend and heard Stanford assigned this as summer reading to incoming freshman (no idea if that's true, but it's a good book)
Blitzscaling Business justification to the bonfire of capital some companies fuel
Stubborn Attachments Tyler Cowen seems smart but I couldn't get through this book, probably because I'm still not over my extreme skepticism about non-intro economics yet
Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance It's a great book and will make you Think About Life, but it's very long
The Design of Everyday Things Sometimes frustratingly repetitive, but overall an excellent intro to all things design. It does view the entire world in a design framework, sort of like how economics professors see everything in terms of prices
